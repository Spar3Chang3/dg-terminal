Subject: Status Update — Field Procedure

Bonnie,

I’m writing to confirm that the procedure carried out last night progressed according to expectations. The reanimation was a success, though I should stress that the results remain preliminary and require continued observation before we can draw any conclusions that would be useful beyond the immediate scope of this operation.

As discussed previously, the subject was recovered intact. Despite the time elapsed prior to our intervention, tissue integrity remained sufficient for the process to take hold. Initial response was delayed longer than the projections suggested, but once activity began it stabilized quickly. Motor response is present, though inconsistent, and there are intermittent lapses that I suspect are tied to the same interference we noted during the first round of tests.

I’ve arranged for the body to be moved to a more secure holding location for the time being. It's the same place we got the other specimens, where you and Molly first met. The current site is no longer appropriate now that the procedure has succeeded; too many people nearby, too many questions if anything were to go wrong again. I will make sure the transfer happens quietly. No one outside our circle has seen anything they shouldn’t.

You asked about identification earlier. The girl’s name has come up in a few of the local reports, but I recommend we avoid attaching anything official to her for now. It’s easier if she remains a missing person rather than a recovered one. That should give us the time we need to determine whether what we’ve done can be repeated.

I will send another report once the subject has been stabilized and we’ve had a chance to run the follow-up examinations. Until then, it would be best if you kept this contained to the usual channels. The fewer people who know what we’re attempting here, the better.

-P.
